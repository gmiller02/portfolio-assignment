Marxists.org Redesign

For an assignment in my UI/UX design class, I was tasked to redesign a poorly designed website. During this project, I completed an entire design process to practice every stage in the journey.

I was given three weeks to complete this assigment, and each week was divided into a checkpoint. I completed a series of lo-fi wireframes the first week, hi-fi wireframes and a style guide during the second week, and completed the website redesign at the end of the third week.

Identifying Usability Problems

Website and page:

I chose to redesign marxists.org because when I polled several close friends of mine about terrible interfaces they have used in the past, two of them named this particular website independently. Both of these friends are history majors, as am I, and I have a particular interest in making sure public archives are accessible to the public.

Finding Problems:

There are several notable issues with the original website. The text itself is incredibly low-resolution. The page does not fill the entire screen lengthwise and if the computer screen is not fully extended to the bottom, you do not see the select boxes. The select boxes visually clash with the rest of the screen. The text colors do not coordinate with the page and seem chosen just because they are bright. The background images are incredibly hard to make out and therefore seem unnecessary, and there is no comprehensible visual hierarchy.

Additionally, the website was not designed with acessibility in mind. Few images have alt text, there is no heading structure or page regions, and the layout table at the bottom is not visible or particularly striking. Additionally, the links to the particular scholarsâ€™ archives have no text and are not visually clickable.

In short, it looks terrible and is difficult to use. With these issues in mind, I began the design process.

Lo-Fi Mockups

Based on the final sketch, I sketched some lo-fi mockups, which incorporated the design of the sketch and the design problems I identified earlier.

Style Guide

My style guide went through two iterations: originally, I had some red elements on the black background, but after conducting some research on accessible color pallates I saw that in keeping the red on black color scheme of the original page, I was not improving on the visibility of the original website. I thus decided to make a more visually striking style guide based on black, white, and shades of grey in order to match the greyscale images retained from the original design.

Takeaways:

The aim of this project was to redesign a webpage in order to develop design skills, identify accessibility issues, and gain coding experience as it relates to web-app interfaces. I learned how to effectively articulate the differences between effective and ineffective design, break down why I made certian design choices, and practice certian basic elements of the design timeline such as mockups, style guides, and ensureing that the designs are responsive.
